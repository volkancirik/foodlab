Filled Artichoke

May 9, 2020

This is an improved version of my mom’s recipe. I’ve never had this recipe in a restaurant, hence the post. Artichokes are so flavorful, you can do few other magical stuff with them, I hope I’ll have time to write those recipes as well. Enjoy!

Step 0: Ingredients

Below is the standard recipe, but you can be more creative with the filling and the broth.

  • 4 artichokes (the leaves on, duh!)
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 2 lemons
  • A handful of chopped dill
  • 1 cup of your favorite kind of rice
  • 1 white onion
  • Half tablespoon red pepper paste
  • Half tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, thyme or oregano, cumin.

Step 1: Simmering


When you take the artichokes out do not forget to wash them with cold water so that you do not burn your hands. Boil water, add a juice of a lemon and simmer artichokes lid on in this mix for 30 minutes.

Step 2: Cleaning


The center has baby leaves that are more like spikes or thorn.


Be careful do not put a whole there.Using a spoon, we cut the centerpiece of the artichoke. . Carve this area with your spoon.

Step 3: Preparing Filling

In a bowl, mix rice, chopped onion, dill (save some for dressing), spices, the pastes, and olive oil. This is where you can be creative. If you are an omnivore you can add ground meat.

Step 4: Filling the Artichoke


You can add more tomato paste to the broth.Fill the inside of each leave and the heart of the artichoke with the filling. But a clove of garlic and lemon ring on top of the heart. Add (prefarebly boiling) water and a juice of a half lemon to the bowl, stir it well. Pour this water/lemon juice/paste broth on top of each artichoke.

Step 5: Cooking

Put them on high heat when the broth starts boiling turn it on low and simmer it for 20 minutes. If the rice is soft, they should be ready.

Step 6: Serving

Serve with yogurt and extra plate for the leave wastes. Use leaves as spoons to eat the filling. Use your lower teeth to bite off the meaty part of each leaves.
