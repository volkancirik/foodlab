Yogurt

May 29, 2020

As an expat I am not sure if I should consider myself as expat or immigrant. , I miss sour Turkish yogurt. The best alternative I could find was Bulgarian yogurt Fun trivia my grandparents from both sides are immigrants from Bulgaria. at Wholefoods. But you can easily make your own sour Turkish yogurt.

Step 0: Ingredients

  • Whole milk as much as your glass container(s) can contain do not use any other kind of milk, what is the point?
  • Tablespoon of starter yogurt
  • Thermometer
  • Glass container(s) with a lid
  • Your favorite blanket

Step 1: Boil the Milk

Boil the milk and pour to your containers. Wait till the temperature drops to 115F.

Normally you wouldn’t have to boil milk to get to fermentation temperature if your house warm. But the bacteria working for us is active at 110F - 115F temperatures. Thus, pouring milk to containers while it’s super hot will heat the containers which will help us keep the temperature in the right range longer.

Step 2: Mix the Starter

Take few tablespoons of milk and mixed it with yogurt till the mixture is smooth in a small bowl. Add this mixture to the milk in 115F and stir it gently. Put the lids on, and cover the containers with a blanket.

Step 3: Wait

Wait for at least 12 hours and put it in the fridge. Do the tasting after the yogurt is cold.

Optional: Drained Yogurt

For many mezes we prepare for Raki tables There should be a blogpost for Turkish Raki tables and my meze recipes we use a dense version of Yogurt. To get the right viscosity for your mezes, you can pour yogurt to a strainer and keep it in the fridge overnight. The next morning you’ll have a perfect drained yogurt. Do not throw away the water (or juice?) add it to your hummus or shakes.

Optional: DIY Yogurt Machine:

The longer the milk is in the right temperature range, the more sour your yogurt will be. So it is essential to have a setup. Below is an image for my DIY yogurt machine. Here I use a box, styrofoam, and aluminium bag, and styrofoam stand to put the containers in. It works like a charm!
